01

Your hormones are working against you

Up to 1 in 6 Nigerian women have PCOS — most undiagnosed. PCOS causes insulin resistance, elevated androgens, and makes fat loss dramatically harder. No generic diet addresses this. We have an entire module dedicated to it.

02

Your portions were never calibrated for Nigerian food

Eba, jollof, egusi, plantain — these are calorie-dense foods eaten in large portions without context. The fix is not elimination — it is the plate method designed specifically for Nigerian meals.

Chronic stress is storing fat on your belly

Cortisol — your stress hormone — signals your body to store fat around the abdomen. The Nigerian woman's stress load (work, family, relationships, financial pressure) is a direct driver of weight gain that no calorie counter addresses.

You've been exercising the wrong way for your body

Hours of cardio with no strength training burns muscle, spikes cortisol, and slows your metabolism. Nigerian women need a specific combination of strength and movement — not punishment sessions at a gym.
